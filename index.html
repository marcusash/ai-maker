> WHAT IS AI MAKER?
Your AI assistant for research, writing, brainstorming, data analysis, design, and communication, in a real window, not a terminal. One agent, 11 skills, five minutes to set up.
Runs inside the GitHub Copilot App. Works everywhere. No admin rights required.
> SETUP GUIDE
1
What you need
  • Windows 11
  • Internet connection
  • A GitHub account, see account setup → if you're not sure which one to use
Upgrading from the old CLI setup? Migration guide →
2
Install
Open PowerShell (Win+X → Terminal) and paste this command. When prompted, choose option 1 (Blue Pill):
>> PASTE INTO POWERSHELL
curl.exe -sSL -o $env:TEMP\install.bat https://github.com/marcusash/ai-maker/releases/download/v3.0.5/install.bat; & $env:TEMP\install.bat
3
Open the GitHub Copilot App
The app opens automatically after install. Or search “GitHub Copilot” in the Start menu.
When asked to sign in:
  • Go to github.com and sign in with your enterprise credentials, your alias_microsoft account
  • The app will show you a code, enter it on GitHub when prompted to authorize the device
  • Follow the prompts to complete the connection
4
Add your project folder
In the App's left rail, find the Sessions section and click the + button next to it. Choose Add local folder and select C:\GitHub\ai-workspace. This is where your AI agent lives, the folder was created during install.
5
Start your first session
Open a new session in the App and say hello. AI maker greets you and asks what you’re working on. All 11 skills are always active, just describe what you need.
6
Try these. Click to copy, then paste into AI maker.
TIP  Hit Win+H to use voice typing. Enter prompts or add context faster.

> WHAT YOU GET
One AI agent with 11 dedicated skills. Describe what you need, it routes automatically.
Canvas
Generates a live HTML page in your browser, dashboards, status boards, trackers, comparison views.
Trigger: “Build me a canvas that shows…” / “Display this in a canvas.”
Vault
Writes notes, decisions, and context to a local file on your PC. Findable and reusable across future sessions.
Trigger: “Save this to my vault as a [decision / note / log entry].”
WorkIQ
Reads your Microsoft 365, email, calendar, Teams, Planner tasks. Returns summaries and answers. Makes no changes.
Trigger: “What’s on my calendar this week?” / “Catch me up on what I missed in Teams.”
Agency
Acts on your Microsoft 365, sends emails, posts to Teams, creates meetings, adds Planner tasks. Your approval is required before anything sends.
Trigger: “Send a Teams message to [person] that says…” / “Create a meeting with…”
Research
Analyzes a document, dataset, or topic and returns structured findings, key patterns, or a brief you can act on.
Trigger: “Research [topic]” / “Analyze this document and tell me what stands out.”
Design
Visual direction, deck structure, layout critique, brand review. Gets your work to a presentation-ready standard.
Trigger: “Give me [N] rules for…” / “Review this design for…”
Writing
Drafts and edits that preserve your voice, emails, announcements, feedback, scripts, talking points.
Trigger: “Write a [type] for [situation].” / “Help me say this better.”
Code
Explains what engineers built in plain English. Helps non-coders evaluate PRs, ask the right questions, and spot red flags.
Trigger: “Explain this PR.” / “What should I ask my engineer about this refactor?”
Data
Interprets metrics, charts, and spreadsheets. Tells you what the numbers mean and what to watch for in a decision.
Trigger: “Analyze this spreadsheet and give me observations.” / “What does this data actually mean?”
Brainstorm
Generates ideas, red-teams plans, plays devil’s advocate, runs pre-mortems. Stress-tests your thinking before you commit.
Trigger: “Brainstorm ideas for…” / “Red-team this plan.” / “What could go wrong?”
Ops
Sprint planning, team communications, meeting prep, status updates, retrospectives. Runs the operational rhythm of your team.
Trigger: “Help me plan this sprint.” / “Write a status update for my team.” / “Prepare me for this 1:1.”
> SOMETHING WRONG?
The installer didn’t finish or something looks off
It’s safe to run again, the installer detects what’s already in place and skips those steps.
irm https://github.com/marcusash/ai-maker/releases/download/v3.0.5/reset.bat -OutFile $env:TEMP\reset.bat; & $env:TEMP\reset.bat
