AI Maker User Guide

Your personal AI partner. Learn what to ask and how it works.

Your First Session

The first time you launch AI Maker, it will run an onboarding interview. This takes about 5 minutes. It asks you questions about how you work, what you need help with, and how you prefer to communicate.

Your answers are saved to C:\AIMaker\profile.md. AI Maker reads this file at the start of every session so you never have to re-explain yourself.

Answer honestly and specifically. The more detail you give, the better AI Maker will adapt to you from day one.

What to Ask AI Maker

Draft or edit communications

"Draft an email to my team explaining why we're pausing the Q2 roadmap review. Keep it direct, not apologetic."

AI Maker knows your communication style from your profile. It will match your tone automatically.

Prepare for tough conversations

"I need to tell my manager that the timeline is unrealistic. Help me prepare: what should I say, what might they push back on, and how do I respond?"

AI Maker will role-play the conversation and help you think through objections before they happen.

Summarize and synthesize

"Here are notes from three meetings this week. Pull out the decisions, the action items, and the things that are stuck."

Paste your notes, Teams transcripts, or email threads. AI Maker will structure them.

Brainstorm and ideate

"I need to come up with 5 ways to reduce onboarding time for new engineers. Don't give me generic answers. Ask me clarifying questions first."

AI Maker will push back if your prompt is vague. It asks questions to understand context before answering.

Build something visual

"Create a slide showing our Q3 priorities: ship the redesign, hire two engineers, launch the beta. Make it look professional but not boring."

AI Maker can generate HTML slides, diagrams, and visuals. It opens them in your browser automatically.

How AI Maker Learns

AI Maker learns from corrections. If it gives you an answer that is not quite right, tell it what to change:

"That email is too formal. Rewrite it like I'm talking to a peer, not a VP."

AI Maker will remember that preference. Next time, it will default to a less formal tone.

You can also update your profile explicitly:

"Update my profile: I prefer bullet points over paragraphs."

What AI Maker Is Not For

Deep technical work: If you need to debug PowerShell scripts, manage GitHub repos, or analyze data, that is not AI Maker's job. That is what the red pill (AI Workbench) is for.

Factual research with citations: AI Maker can brainstorm and synthesize, but if you need fact-checked research with sources, use a research-focused tool or the red pill setup.

Next Steps

Try this today:

1. Ask AI Maker to help you with something you are actually working on right now. Do not test it with a fake question.

2. If the answer is not quite right, tell it what to change. Watch how it adapts.

3. After 3-5 sessions, AI Maker will know your style. That is when it gets good.
